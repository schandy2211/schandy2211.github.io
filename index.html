🧬 About

I am a postdoctoral scholar at UC Berkeley and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, working with Prof. Teresa Head-Gordon. UC Berkeley and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, working with Prof. Teresa Head-Gordon. My research combines quantum chemistry, molecular simulation, and machine learning to model complex aqueous and catalytic systems. Previously, I earned my Ph.D. in Chemistry from Indiana University Bloomington under Prof. Krishnan Raghavachari, developing fragmentation-based and machine learning approaches for large biomolecular systems.

Vision: I am broadly interested in research at the interface of machine learning and chemistry — building physics-informed frameworks that connect quantum accuracy with chemical scalability. My long-term goal is to design interpretable ML models and simulation tools that accelerate materials and molecular discovery in catalysis, solvation, and electronic structure modeling.

🔬 Current Research

  • Nitrogen Reduction at Magnetite–Water Interfaces: Conducting ab initio molecular dynamics and free energy simulations to understand ambient-condition ammonia formation; results featured as an ALS Science Highlight.
  • ML-Enhanced Potential Energy Surfaces: Building deep learning models (PyTorch) for ion–water and water–water clusters, improving the accuracy and transferability of polarizable force fields.
  • Dielectric Suppression in Electrolyte Systems: Quantifying ion-induced dielectric decrement using AMOEBA and AIMD simulations with hybrid DFT (SCAN, PBE0-D3).

🧫 Previous Work

  • MIM-ML Framework: Developed a fragment-based random forest model for NMR prediction in nucleic acids, achieving DFT-level accuracy at reduced cost.
  • CO₂ Reduction Catalysis: Modeled Re and Mo nanographene catalysts to identify low-energy PCET pathways, supporting experimental work achieving >90% Faradaic efficiency.
  • Odorant Antagonism Mechanisms: Used AlphaFold2 and docking workflows to propose dual-antagonism binding in Drosophila Or56a.
