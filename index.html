Research Description

During my postdoctoral work at Arizona State University's Biodesign Institute, I focused on developing reactive machine learning interatomic potentials, which significantly enhance our ability to simulate and predict molecular interactions. This work is pivotal in various fields, including materials science and nanotechnology.

Additionally, I investigated the mechanisms of amide bond hydrolysis, providing insights into essential biochemical processes relevant to pharmaceuticals and biology. My research also delved into prebiotic chemistry, exploring the fundamental reactions that could lead to the origin of life. These studies are critical for understanding how complex biological molecules can form under early Earth conditions.
