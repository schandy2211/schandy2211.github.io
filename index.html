Sruthy K. Chandy

Computational Chemist | Machine Learning Scientist | UC Berkeley / LBNL Postdoctoral Scholar

🧬 About

I am a postdoctoral scholar at UC Berkeley and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, where I combine quantum chemistry, molecular simulations, and machine learning to study catalytic and aqueous systems. My research focuses on developing physics-informed and data-driven frameworks for molecular property prediction, catalysis, and interface dynamics. Previously, I completed my Ph.D. in Chemistry at Indiana University in the Raghavachari Group.

🔬 Research Highlights

  • MIM-ML: Developed a fragment-based, physics-informed ML framework for NMR prediction in biomolecules.
  • Hybrid ML Force-Field Corrections: Built delta-learning models bridging polarizable force-field and DFT accuracy.
  • Fe3O4–Water–N2 Interfaces: Combined AIMD and DFT to uncover mechanisms for ambient ammonia synthesis.
  • Dielectric Properties of Polarizable Water: Performed long-timescale simulations to quantify dielectric decrement and solvation structure.

📚 Selected Publications
