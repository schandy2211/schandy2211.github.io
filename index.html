Computational Chemist | Machine Learning Scientist | UC Berkeley / LBNL Postdoctoral Scholar

About

I am a postdoctoral scholar at UC Berkeley and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, where I integrate quantum chemistry, molecular simulations, and machine learning to model complex chemical systems. My research focuses on developing physics-informed and data-driven frameworks for molecular property prediction, catalysis, and aqueous interface dynamics.

Research Highlights

  • MIM-ML: Developed a fragment-based, physics-informed ML framework for NMR prediction in biomolecules.
  • Hybrid ML Force-Field Corrections: Built delta-learning models bridging polarizable force-field and DFT accuracies.
  • Fe3O4–Water–N2 Interfaces: Combined AIMD and DFT to reveal reaction mechanisms for ambient ammonia synthesis.
  • Dielectric Properties of Polarizable Water: Performed long-timescale simulations to quantify dielectric decrement and solvation structure.

Selected Publications

  • J. Am. Chem. Soc., 2025 – Lead Author: “Ammonia Synthesis under Ambient Conditions via Fe₃O₄–Water Interfaces.”
  • J. Chem. Theory Comput., 2022 – Lead Author: “Fragment-Based Machine Learning for NMR Prediction (MIM-ML).”
